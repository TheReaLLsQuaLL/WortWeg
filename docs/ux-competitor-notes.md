# WortWeg UX Competitor Notes

Purpose: capture high-level UX principles for WortWeg without copying competitor screens, text, exercises, visual layouts, or proprietary flows.

## Duolingo-Style Principles

- Keep the first session short and obvious: one question or one action per screen.
- Make the next lesson visually dominant so the user rarely has to decide what to do.
- Show progress and streaks, but keep them compact so they motivate rather than distract.
- Use a simple path metaphor: completed, current, next, locked.
- Reward completion immediately with a clear next action.

## Babbel-Style Principles

- Start from user goals such as travel, work, daily life, or exam preparation.
- Keep lessons practical and scenario-based.
- Make course structure visible so users trust the sequence.
- Prefer short grammar explanations connected to the current exercise.
- Let users understand why a lesson exists before they start it.

## Busuu-Style Principles

- Respect level awareness: self-level plus optional placement makes the plan feel personal.
- Explain the recommended starting level briefly and gently.
- Show a study-plan feeling: daily time, target level, first milestone.
- Make progress feel CEFR-based without overwhelming beginners.
- Keep plan editing available, but not central on the first Home screen.

## Memrise-Style Principles

- Make language feel alive through short speech and listening prompts.
- Encourage speaking early, even if scoring is still mocked.
- Use memorable, compact vocabulary encounters rather than long lists on Home.
- Keep audio/speech actions lightweight and easy to find.
- Use real-life contexts while keeping A0/A1 German simple.

## WortWeg Application

- Turkish explanations stay first; German examples stay short.
- Wolli guides the roadmap, but does not over-explain.
- Home answers three questions only: What do I do today? Where am I? What comes next?
- Detailed curriculum and plan data remain available behind Plan and Curriculum screens.
- A0/A1 playable lessons are foregrounded; A2+ remains clearly marked as coming soon.
